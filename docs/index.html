01 — Overview

Three Systems,
One Composited Frame

The project establishes a real-time Virtual Production pipeline in which three independent technical layers operate simultaneously and are visible in a single composited output frame. Each layer builds on the previous: depth-correct occlusion requires camera tracking; embodied interaction requires the calibrated skeletal stream that occlusion depends on.

01 / SIGNAL PROCESSING

Camera Tracking & Occlusion

Base-free 6-DoF camera tracking via ZED SLAM. Per-pixel depth-correct AR occlusion through a Spout-streamed depth map reprojected into UE5 Composure. Stencil-based edge softness per AR object.

02 / KINEMATIC EXTRACTION

Skeletal Denoising

ZED BODY_38 38-joint tracking at 30 Hz. 1 Euro Filter applied per-joint to suppress geometric sensor noise while preserving transient dynamics. LMA Expansiveness metric computed offline as analytical validation.

03 / EMBODIED INTERACTION

Kinematic AR

Four LMA Effort descriptors computed from the live skeletal stream drive CG element behaviour. Movement quality — not position — modulates the virtual environment. Two switchable operating modes with no level reload.

02 — LMA Descriptors

Four Qualities.
Continuous. Real-Time.

The system does not classify gesture types or respond to body position. It computes four continuous scalar descriptors from the live ZED skeletal stream each tick, each corresponding to one of Laban's four Effort factors. The eight canonical LMA Action Drives emerge as regions in the resulting four-dimensional quality space rather than being explicitly detected.

LMA Time — Sudden ↔ Sustained

Effort

Weighted RMS velocity of key limb joints — wrists, elbows, shoulders, spine, head. Measures the energetic urgency of movement. The same physical position produces any Effort value depending on how the body arrived there.

→ Niagara · particle emission rate & velocity
LMA Space — Expanded ↔ Contracted

Expansiveness

Maximum distance from either wrist to spine_2. The maximum of two wrists captures kinesphere reach correctly — the largest spatial gesture defines the body's spatial attitude. Force direction follows the wrist-to-spine vector, not omnidirectionally.

→ MPC · WPO deformation frequency · RadialForce radius
LMA Weight — Strong ↔ Light

Weight

Acceleration magnitude across key joints — rate of velocity change. Independent of Effort: a body can move fast with low acceleration (quick drift) or fast with high acceleration (punch). High Weight means force is being exerted or absorbed.

→ MPC · WPO surface bend amplitude
LMA Flow — Free ↔ Bound

Flow

Rolling standard deviation of velocity delta over a 30-frame window. High sigma — velocity is erratic, unpredictable — reads as Free. Low sigma — velocity changes predictably — reads as Bound. Serves simultaneously as the internal smoothing control variable.

→ Niagara · attractor strength  ·  MPC · surface noise scale

03 — Demonstration

A Grid That Listens
to How You Move

The demonstration places the performer at the centre of a three-dimensional grid of physics-simulated cubic objects in an LED volume. The grid is permanently anchored to the performer's pelvis joint, keeping the body at the centre of the formation regardless of movement through the stage.

Cubes in front of the performer are occluded by the depth comparison pipeline — the body correctly appears in front of them. Cubes behind are hidden behind the body. A camera rotating around the performer continuously renegotiates these depth relationships, demonstrating that occlusion is genuinely three-dimensional.

The grid responds to movement quality, not position. An arm thrust forward with high force pushes the grid forward along the arm's direction vector. A slow expansion opens the grid outward over a wide radius. A sudden explosive movement scatters the cubes and floods the space with particles.

The system reads the body's expressive state, not its location. A stationary performer exerting high muscular tension produces a different grid response than the same position held with no tension.
04 — Smoothing Models

Three Philosophies
of Response

Geometric sensor noise from ZED BODY_38 joint position estimation is suppressed using the 1 Euro Filter per joint before descriptor computation. On top of this, the computed descriptor scalars pass through a calibrated adaptive interpolation stage. Three mapping models are available, selectable at runtime — each corresponding to a distinct movement tradition and LMA quality.

Model 01

Linear

Conservative proportional response. Tracks the descriptor signal faithfully. Never overreacts. Every movement registers with appropriate weight.

LMA: Sustained + Bound
Classical Ballet · Cecchetti · RAD · Vaganova · Neoclassical (Balanchine, Forsythe)
Model 02

Sigmoid

Non-linear self-regulating response. Most sensitive at the transition zone between stability and instability — where movement quality is most expressively complex.

LMA: Transitional + Free
Contemporary · Limón · Release Technique · Contact Improvisation · Cunningham
Model 03

Piecewise

Explicit sigma breakpoints corresponding to five LMA velocity tiers. Distinct behavioural states with calibrated threshold transitions. Recognises when a qualitative tier has been crossed.

LMA: Sudden + Strong · Bound between states
Hip-Hop · Popping · Locking · Flamenco · Butoh · Breaking
05 — Repositories

System Architecture
Across Four Repos

The project is structured as four independent repositories, each with a clearly scoped responsibility. The offline analysis and calibration repos feed configuration outputs that the runtime repo consumes at startup.

VP_AR_full_System
Offline pipeline: temporal alignment, spatial calibration, kinematic extraction and 1 Euro filtering. This page.

_runtime
UE5 C++ runtime: camera tracking, compositing, occlusion, EI descriptor components, Niagara, mode switching.
